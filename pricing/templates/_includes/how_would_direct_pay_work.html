INSURANCE' text="TRADITIONALLY
Insurers negotiate discounted rates with doctors in their network. Doctors charge different amounts to different insurers for the same procedures, but these rates are not publicly available.

WITH DIRECT PAY
Health insurance would resemble auto insurance more - insurance estimates the cost of a procedure and sends a check to the patient who compares prices and chooses a doctor." %}
{% include '_includes/about_widget.html' with icon='fa fa-building-o' title='SELF-FUNDED
EMPLOYER' text="TRADITIONALLY
Employers generally hire a Preferred Provider Organization (PPO) which negotiates discounted rates with doctors on the employer's behalf. Exmployers pay out of their operating budgets.

WITH DIRECT PAY
Employers would partner with doctors on the basis of their publicly available prices and then pay them directly. Employers would still pay medical bills out of their operating budgets." %}
{% include '_includes/about_widget.html' with icon='fa fa-flag' title='MEDICARE /
MEDICAID' text="TRADITIONALLY
The government determines how much to reimburse doctors for all procedures. Rates are often modest, leading some doctors to refuse to accept such coverage.

WITH DIRECT PAY
If most doctors posted their prices, the government may set reimbursement rates based on the average regional price of the procedure, rather than from the American Medical Association." %}
{% include '_includes/about_widget.html' with icon='fa fa-money' title='OUT OF
POCKET' text="TRADITIONALLY
Patients pay doctors for the procedures directly. In the past, uninsured patients' bills would be based on the artifically inflated prices doctors used to begin negotiations with insurers.

WITH DIRECT PAY
Patients still pay doctors directly but since price lists are public, regional providers can complete on price, quality and reputation." %}
